# linkedin-ads-bidding-budget

Use this skill to manage LinkedIn campaign pacing, set bidding strategies,
and prevent the platform from burning budget inefficiently.

## Bidding Strategies

### 1. Manual CPC (Default for Launch)
Take control of your cost per click.
**When to use:** Launching new campaigns, testing new creatives, strict CPL constraints.
**Execution:** Set your bid 10-15% below LinkedIn's "Recommended" range. Adjust up slowly if the campaign doesn't spend its daily budget.

### 2. Maximum Delivery (Automated)
LinkedIn spends your full daily budget to get maximum results.
**When to use:** You have proven creatives, a tightly constrained ABM audience (under 20k), and need to ensure your ads are seen by everyone on the list.
**Warning:** Can result in extremely high CPCs on broad audiences.

### 3. Manual CPM
Bidding per 1,000 impressions.
**When to use:** Upper funnel brand awareness with video ads, where clicks are secondary to reach and frequency.

## Budgeting Framework

### Minimum Viable Spend
- Minimum recommended total budget: $3,000/month.
- Minimum daily budget per campaign: $25-$50.
- If you have $3k/mo, run a maximum of 2-3 campaigns simultaneously. Consolidate audiences rather than spreading budget too thin.

### The "Cost per Outcome" Rule
To properly test a campaign, you need at least 10x the target Cost Per Action (CPA) in budget.
If target Cost Per Demo = $250, you need $2,500 in test budget before judging the campaign a failure.

## Optimization Cadence

1. **Days 1-3:** Monitor spend pacing only. Increase manual bids if spend is < 20% of daily budget.
2. **Days 7-14:** Review CTRs. Pause creatives under 0.35% CTR.
3. **Days 30+:** Review Cost per Lead / Cost per Pipeline. Shift budget from high CPL campaigns to low CPL campaigns.

## Output Contract
When advising on budget/bidding, provide:
- The recommended bidding strategy (Manual vs Max Delivery)
- Suggested starting CPC/CPM relative to industry average
- Daily budget allocation across the proposed campaigns
- The "kill criteria" (when to pause a campaign based on spend vs results)
